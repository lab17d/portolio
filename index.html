LIS 4351: User Experience Design

Fall 2020

This course is an overview of the user experience design process. It is comprised of group design sprints where the professor choses an interface that students are challenged to redesign. Periodically during the course, students were surprised with unannounced timed intervals to design or modify objects based on the lecture taught that day.

Design Sprint 2

Case Study

  • The second design sprint students were tasked to redesign the Crestron Interface. This is a system utilized by professors to control classroom projectors.
  • My group came up with both wireframe concept utilizing InVision and a final design built within Canva.
  • Our design encompassed a touch screen in keeping with the original interface. My workgroup updated the design to resemble an app seen typically on mobile devices and gave the professor the ease of adjustable classroom lighting while being conducive with the projection screen/learning environment.

COM 4470: Desktop Multimedia

Fall 2019

The course curriculum was set up in a fashion where we, as students, roleplayed and represented a marketing firm that subsequently was hired for a print and web-based campaign. The class requirements included our ability to introduce three of Adobe Creative Suite elements.

Throughout the course we were challenged to:

  • Articulate a comprehensive project proposal completed with brief outlines of both projects.
  • Draft a print based campaign using Adobe InDesign and PhotoShop.
  • Design and develop a website for the company using Adobe Dreamweaver.

I built this website for multiple reasons, most importantly to gain experience. What better subject matter that I have knowledge of, than myself! Yes, I have built websites before. I wanted to create something of value to showcase my skill set. I incorporated ideas I learned in class and compiled them all into this website. To further my knowledge of web development, I studied the Bootstrap website and watched various YouTube tutorials to come up with this final product.

I created a wireframe with Invision. I curated both the cover and section header graphics on Canva. As a resource, I used Giphy to make all the gifs found throughout this site. When it came to programming, I took to w3Schools for tutorials and Bootstrap 4 for documentation. Finally, to upload this finished project, I used Github to publish.
